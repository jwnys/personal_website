About Me

I'm a research scientist with more than nine years' experience working at the intersection of quantum many‑body physics, machine learning and high‑performance computing. I develop scalable algorithms — both classical and quantum — for simulating electronic matter and quantum dynamics and translate research into robust code. Recently, I have advanced neural‑quantum‑state methods and variational time‑evolution schemes at ETH Zürich and EPFL. My background spans leading cross‑disciplinary teams, industry collaborations and grant‑funded research programmes.

Projects & Experience

Senior Research Assistant – ETH Zürich (10/2024 – Present)

Developing computational methods for strongly correlated materials and quantum device simulation (digital & analog); building high‑performance code for large‑scale many‑body simulations across multi‑GPU platforms.

Post‑doctoral Researcher – EPFL (10/2021 – 09/2024)

Designed novel time‑propagator expansions and variational time‑evolution methods for electronic matter; built neural network architectures for quantum systems; secured partnership funding with Microsoft Research and delivered ML techniques for 2D materials.

Principal Investigator, AI4Science – IMEC & University of Antwerp (10/2020 – 09/2021)

Led research projects on scientific machine learning, topological data analysis and causal machine learning; promoted to principal investigator and supervised a PhD student through cross‑institutional collaborations.

Co‑founder & Head of Machine Learning – Boltzmann (2018 – 2020)

Led a ten‑person ML consulting team delivering R&D projects, launched an NLP anomaly‑detection product adopted across the Belgian bookkeeping industry and oversaw acquisition by Silverfin.

Scientific Advisor – BioClue (2019 – 2020)

Prototyped a DNA sequencing and search‑and‑retrieval model using HYFT patterns, contributing to external investment and subsequent acquisition by IPA.
