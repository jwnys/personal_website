About

I'm a research scientist with around a decade of experience designing machine learning algorithms and numerical methods for hard scientific problems. My work sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, quantum physics, and high-performance computing — I build neural architectures and algorithms for complex, structured data, and I scale them across multi-GPU clusters. I move between modern machine learning (generative models, graph neural networks, attention, LLM agents) and computational quantum science (variational methods, tensor networks, Monte Carlo, quantum algorithms), and I like problems where the two meet.

I'm currently a senior researcher at ETH Zürich. Before that I was a postdoctoral researcher at EPFL (2021–2024), a researcher and group leader at IMEC and the University of Antwerp (2020–2021), and a postdoctoral fellow at Ghent University (2019–2020), where I also did my PhD in theoretical physics (2014–2018). Alongside the PhD I picked up an MSc in Artificial Intelligence from KU Leuven (2020, summa cum laude, part-time), on top of an MSc and BSc in Physics and Astronomy from Ghent (summa cum laude, top of class).

Outside academia I've been an entrepreneur: I co-founded Boltzmann, an ML R&D startup, served as scientific advisor to the biotech company BioStrand (later acquired by IPA), and have delivered machine-learning R&D for clients in fintech, biotech, retail, and accounting. I'm based in Zürich.

Research

A non-exhaustive tour of what I work on. Linked papers below are representative; the full list lives on the publications page.

AI for science & Scientific machine learning

I design neural architectures — equivariant graph neural networks, attention-based models, and physics-informed networks — for high-dimensional scientific problems, with a particular focus on neural-network wave functions and variational Monte Carlo for strongly interacting quantum systems. Recent threads include generative models trained with optimal-transport ideas (Wasserstein Quantum Monte Carlo, NeurIPS 2023), deep learning for time-dependent many-body dynamics (Nature Communications 2024), message-passing wave functions for the homogeneous electron gas (PRB 2024), accurate descriptions of ultra-cold Fermi gases (Comm. Physics 2024), and LLM-based multi-agent systems for automating parts of the scientific discovery loop.

Quantum simulation & quantum computing

I design algorithms for digital and analog quantum devices — fermion-to-qudit mappings (arXiv 2024), stabilizer-based methods for fermion dynamics with reduced non-Clifford gate counts (with IBM Research), and analyses of topological entanglement entropy on Rydberg analogue simulators (Nature Physics 2025). I collaborate with industrial partners including NVIDIA and IBM Research.

Numerical methods & HPC

Underneath the model design sits a steady focus on scalable numerics: Monte Carlo, tensor networks, optimization algorithms, and distributed multi-GPU training in JAX and CUDA on Slurm-based HPC environments. I'm a contributor to NetKet, a JAX-based open-source toolbox for many-body quantum systems (SciPost 2022).
